# Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

aiMed exists to promote transparency. We extend that principle to how we treat each other. Everyone participating in the aiMed project — whether contributing code, documentation, domain profiles, or discussion — deserves a respectful, harassment-free experience.

## Our Standards

**Be welcoming.** AIMED is for everyone — developers, writers, academics, musicians, lawyers, and anyone else who creates. Welcome people regardless of their background, experience level, or chosen tools.

**Be constructive.** Disagree with ideas, not people. Propose alternatives when you critique. Focus on what's best for the standard and its users.

**Be transparent.** Fitting for a transparency standard — be honest about your motivations, affiliations, and conflicts of interest when proposing changes.

## Unacceptable Behavior

- Harassment, intimidation, or discrimination of any kind
- Personal attacks or inflammatory language
- Publishing others' private information without consent
- Sustained disruption of discussion or process
- Any behavior that would make a reasonable person feel unwelcome

## Enforcement

Project maintainers will address unacceptable behavior by removing comments, blocking contributors, or taking other action as appropriate. Instances of unacceptable behavior may be reported to the project maintainers.

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies in all project spaces — issues, pull requests, discussions, and any other channel associated with aiMed.
